Leonardo da Vinci

Renowned Italian Renaissance artist, inventor, and polymath, known for masterpieces like *Mona Lisa* and *The Last Supper*.

Vincent van Gogh

Dutch Post-Impressionist painter famous for his bold colors and emotional depth, particularly in works like *Starry Night*.

Pablo Picasso

Spanish painter and sculptor, co-founder of Cubism, known for works like *Guernica* and *Les Demoiselles d'Avignon*.

Claude Monet

French Impressionist painter best known for his serene landscapes, especially *Water Lilies* and *Impression, Sunrise*.

Frida Kahlo

Mexican painter known for her surrealist and symbolic self-portraits that explore themes of identity and pain.

Salvador Dalí

Spanish surrealist painter famous for his eccentric, dream-like imagery, such as in *The Persistence of Memory*.

Johannes Vermeer

Dutch Baroque painter renowned for his delicate and intimate depictions of domestic life, including *Girl with a Pearl Earring*.

Rembrandt van Rijn

Dutch master painter known for his portraits and use of light, particularly in *The Night Watch* and *Self-Portraits*.

Andy Warhol

American artist and leading figure in the Pop Art movement, famous for his silkscreen paintings, like *Campbell's Soup Cans*.

Jackson Pollock

American abstract expressionist known for his drip paintings that emphasize spontaneous, dynamic movement.

Georgia O'Keeffe

American modernist painter celebrated for her large-scale flowers and desert landscapes, like *Black Iris* and *Sky Above Clouds*.

Michelangelo

Italian Renaissance sculptor and painter, known for works like *David*, *The Sistine Chapel Ceiling*, and *The Creation of Adam*.

Edvard Munch

Norwegian painter and printmaker best known for his iconic painting *The Scream*, expressing emotional anguish.

Mark Rothko

American abstract expressionist known for his large fields of color and emotionally resonant works, such as *No. 61 (Rust and Blue)*.

Caravaggio

Italian Baroque artist known for his dramatic use of light and realistic depiction of subjects, particularly in *The Calling of Saint Matthew*.

Gustav Klimt

Austrian symbolist painter best known for his ornate, gold-leafed works like *The Kiss* and *Portrait of Adele Bloch-Bauer I*.

Botticelli

Italian painter of the Early Renaissance, famous for works like *The Birth of Venus* and *Primavera*.

Henri Matisse

French painter known for his use of bold color and innovative techniques in works like *The Red Studio* and *The Snail*.

Diego Rivera

Mexican muralist famous for his large-scale public works depicting Mexican history and culture.

Mary Cassatt

American Impressionist painter known for her sensitive depictions of women and children, such as *The Child's Bath*.

Edouard Manet

French painter and one of the pioneers of modern art, known for works like *Olympia* and *Luncheon on the Grass*.

Paul Cézanne

French post-impressionist painter whose work laid the foundations for the transition from 19th-century artistic concepts to modern art.

Henri Toulouse-Lautrec

French Post-Impressionist artist known for his depictions of Parisian nightlife, such as in *At the Moulin Rouge*.

Wassily Kandinsky

Russian painter and pioneer of abstract art, known for his vivid, geometric, and spiritual compositions.

Roy Lichtenstein

American pop artist known for his comic-strip-style paintings, particularly *Hopeless* and *Whaam!*, which use bold lines and primary colors.
